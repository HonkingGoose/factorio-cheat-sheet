There are 2 systems for trains/stations:
  • Pass through stations with single headed trains - stations are built in the middle of the track such that after the train has stopped it continues forward
  • Terminal stations with double headed trains - stations are built at the end of the track such that after the train has stopped it reverses
Stations and interrupts can be composed with the following identifiers:
  • or (load/unload)
  • or (fluid/cargo wagon)
  • [#locomotives in front]-[#wagons]-[#locomotives behind]
Generally can use the following configurations for trains for an average game (remove the locos behind if not using terminal stations):
  • 2-4-2 trains for ore
  • 1-2-1 train for oil
  • 1-1-1 train for acid (uranium mine)

Signals tutorial

When building rails the general rule is:
  • "Use chain signal before intersection, block signal after" - chain in block out
  • Space signals to account for the longest train. to prevent deadlocks
